What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where students individually or collaboratively create innovative solutions with technology, often using hardware, software, or both! Students present and share their ideas with other participants and can be awarded with prizes for their work. In addition, due to many workshops and experienced mentors, a hackathon is a great learning opportunity and experience. Overall, hackathons are an excellent event for any student with an interest in computer science and programming to attend, no matter the skill level.

FAQs

How long is RidgeHacks?

14 hours from check-in to the end of closing ceremonies. Hacking is 12 hours long.

What do I need to bring?

A laptop/computer, an ID, and any hardware you plan on using in your hack.

How do teams work?

Teams are composed of up to 4 people. We can help you find a team at the event if you don't have one.

What can I make?

Anything you want - hardware, software, or a mix!

What can I win?

Prizes are TBA but we promise they will be amazing.

Is there travel reimbursement?

Sorry, but we cannot reimburse travel expenses this year. However, you can apply for Execute Big's Travel Grant so you can attend RidgeHacks without paying for travel.
